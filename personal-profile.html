What do the results of these tests mean for you?

I think these results accurately reflect my behaviour in typical work situations and in formal contexts. For example, I tend to be reserved and prone to hiding from the spotlight; yet it is mainly when I am with those who I have a formal relationship with, such as bosses and colleagues. Once I know them better, I open up and become bolder in expressing my points of view. I know that I thrive when creativity is encouraged and welcome, and I can produce better results and unconventional solutions. I can communicate passionately and convincingly when I can trust those around me.

Fortunately, my life credo and default attitude is to trust people unless they prove themselves untrustworthy. I like establishing new relationships and listening to people. It is no accident that my other careers are in coaching / counseling and language interpreting, both of which involve a lot of communication with people. Even though I am not an extravert, I have practised public speaking enough to come across confidently.

I am not very well organised and appreciate non-intrusive reminders to plan and prioritise my work. As long as the planning horizon is not too far off and the scope of work in front of me is not too overwhelming, I usually manage to be quite productive. When working on individual projects, I appreciate working with another person to help me stay on track.

As a learner, I do indeed usually prefer visual and auditory learning, although I am not limited to these two styles. I like watching and following visual courses, where instructors show how to do something and I can follow along. When it comes to less hands-on subjects, I like listening to and learning from audiobooks, audiocourses and podcasts. I also like attending live offline workshops and trainings when I can practise and be supervised and given feedback by an experienced instructor.

While I love reading books, I have found that I am not enjoying learning skills from them. I like them for prompting deep thinking about philosophical and abstract concepts, as well as for learning soft skills.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

I know from experience how my personality would influence my behaviour in a team. Until I get to know my colleagues, I tend to quietly listen, watch and learn. I am not ready to give input or feedback unless I am well familiar with facts and context. Once I am satisfied that I have enough such information, I am happy to actively contribute although I prefer others to be in the spotlight. I like working in solitude and with as little disturbance and interference as possible, yet it does not mean that I do not maintain sufficient communication with the team. I just do not like being in a noisy and busy environment, always surrounded by people because I find it difficult to concentrate and produce my best work. I am conscientious and productive, and being relationship-oriented and socially-aware, I like helping both my colleagues and clients by offering my knowledge and skills.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

If I am the one forming a team, then I know I would need to complement or counteract my weaknesses by recruiting a team mate who is strong where I am weak. I guess the same should go for the teams, in which I am participating as a member. I need to be given enough autonomy and the time and environment to concentrate. I would prefer to be given space to observe and gather facts at first but I will play full out once I feel some solid ground under my feet. Perhaps a frontline leadership role may not be for me, yet I am happy and good at encouraging and even guiding teammates from the back. I would not be a good fit in a team, where micro-managing is part of the culture. I do not do well in environments with open desk and shared workspaces so it is best if I am given at least a modicum of privacy. I am quite methodical once I know what I am doing, so I can be responsible for writing and maintaining some of the back-end code. I am not very good at design, so I should not be given tasks involving visual presentation and visual appearance of the product.
